Brand Identity

Six logo directions for Talle Furniture Mart

Each option is shown on a white background (navbar context) and on a dark background (footer / shipping label context). They're all pure SVG — sharp at every size, print-safe, easy to tweak. Pick the one you want and I'll wire it in everywhere the text logo currently appears.

Refined classics

1

Architectural mark + wordmark

Modern

Stylised chair line in a charcoal rounded square + tracked TALLE wordmark + thin rule + FURNITURE MART sub-line. Red accent dot used as a precision detail, not as a fill.

Navbar header, app icon, social media avatar, email signatures. Reads cleanly even at favicon size.

Confident · architectural · single weight · no decoration. Furniture-design firm energy, not retail.

2

Editorial wordmark only

Minimal

Pure Playfair Display "Talle" in display weight + hairline rule + tracked FURNITURE MART small caps. Single red dot as accent, nothing else.

Printed invoices, business cards, newsletters, magazine-style headers. Doesn't compete with hero imagery.

Quiet · luxury · editorial — like a high-end furniture house catalogue. Maximum confidence with minimum ink.

3

Heritage seal + wordmark

Heritage Brass

Three-ring brass seal with "T" monogram and curved "TALLE FURNITURE MART · EST. 2010 · MUMBAI" text on the rim. Brass gradient (not red) for warmth + maturity.

Shipping label stamp, invoice header, About page, hangtag stickers, certificate of warranty.

Royal Warrant · craftsman maison · established. Earns trust before the first chair leaves the workshop.

Premium new concepts

4

Bold sans wordmark with terminal dot

Confident Steelcase-style

TALLE set in Poppins Black, ultra-tight letter spacing, followed by a precise red dot as a full-stop. "CHAIR SPECIALISTS · MUMBAI" sub-line, tracked and quiet.

Big bold placements — billboards, the navbar, social-media cover photos, packaging. The dot adds personality without adding clutter.

Confident · contemporary · declarative. Inspired by Steelcase / Vitra / Hay identity work.

5

Lowercase serif + chair monogram

Soft Knoll-style

Charcoal square monogram containing a single-line chair drawing topped with a small red dot. "talle" in lowercase Playfair beside it — softer and more domestic than uppercase.

Brands aiming to feel approachable rather than corporate — boutique furniture houses, B2C-leaning placements, Instagram bio, gift cards.

Soft · domestic · refined. Inspired by Knoll, Carl Hansen, &Tradition.

6

T-monogram chair construction

Clever Negative space

The white "T" inside the charcoal square is also a chair silhouette — the horizontal is the seat, the vertical is the back. Two red dots underneath read as castor wheels. Letter, chair, and base in one mark.

Standalone mark when you don't have room for the full wordmark — favicon, profile picture, embroidery on packaging, embossing on business cards.

Clever · architectural · scales infinitely. Negative-space play, instantly memorable.
